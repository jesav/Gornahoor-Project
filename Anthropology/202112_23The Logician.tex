\begin{quotex}
INTPs are philosophical innovators, fascinated by logical analysis, systems, and design. They are preoccupied with theory, and search for the universal law behind everything they see. They want to understand the unifying themes of life, in all their complexity. INTPs are detached, analytical observers who can seem oblivious to the world around them because they are so deeply absorbed in thought. They spend much of their time in their own heads: exploring concepts, making connections, and seeking understanding of how things work. To the Logician, life is an ongoing inquiry into the mysteries of the universe.

\end{quotex}

Another interesting note that had passed through my mind when examining MBTI was that its functions are based off of the Jungian functions of Sensing , Thinking , Feeling , and Intuition . In other words , Sensing [physical fact] , Thinking [formal logic] , Feeling [moral logic] , Intuition [intuition] .

Now , if we grant the innovation that myers and briggs proposed of `introversion' and `extroversion' and assuming that our Dear Friend preferred his intuition to be internal (introverted), then the MBTI system would dictate , if mapped out to this move from sense perception to intuition , would follow this path: extroverted sensing , introverted thinking , extroverted feeling , introverted intuition .

This pathing , if stacked by ``superiority-inferiority" would yield: Ni , Fe , Ti , Se (the shorthand for the above mentioned functions) .
